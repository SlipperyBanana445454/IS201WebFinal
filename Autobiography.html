Autobiography

My name is Cristopher, and I am from South Africa, but most people just call me Cris. I grew up in a small town about 45km (28 miles) outside of Johannesburg called Heidelberg with my older brother and sister. Growing up, I did almost every sport that was available for me to do—from field hockey to cross country, I did it all. But there was one sport where my performance outshined the rest, and that was swimming.

I started swimming competitively when I was 8 years old, and I started training when I was about 6 years old. During my first 9 years of swimming, I swam in my hometown Heidelberg for a small local club. After that, I moved to a swim coach in Johannesburg, where I swam for the next 4 years before deciding to come to America. As a youngster, I achieved some insane results -- from regional records to national records. I even had a streak of being national age group champion for 8 years straight. But naturally, as you get older, performance and improvement start to level out.

In August 2023, I visited my family, who stayed in Alabama at the time, and during my 2-week stay, my life felt like hell. I struggled to keep in contact with my friends and family back home due to the change in time zone, and this just made me feel alone, depressed, and demotivated to do anything—especially train. After my two-week stay, when I returned to South Africa, I decided that my relationship with my family is more important to me than pursuing my lifelong dream.

My father sadly got diagnosed with cancer back in 2020, and in 2021, he lost his battle. Losing a parent is a haunting experience, and it never seems to escape you. While I was in Alabama back in 2023, one thing kept going through my mind, eating away at my joy, and that was, “what if something happens to my mom or siblings while I'm out of the country studying one day?” The fear of the possibility that something might happen to them, or that my relationship with them would fade with time, and that I might not be able to get back home in time if something does happen, was enough to make me want to give up on my dreams just to have some peace of mind.

After being set on my decision for over a year to stay in South Africa, it all changed on one random night in December. I had a falling out with a close friend, and that was the catalyst that allowed years' worth of bundled-up emotion and thoughts to finally come out. This event made me sit down with my family and explain to them the real reason why I gave up on my dream of competing in the NCAA. Talking to them about my fears allowed me to overcome them and break free from living a life run by my own fears and insecurities. Luckily for me, the recruiting window was still open. Even though many sports on other teams' rosters were full, I still reached out and got a couple of responses from teams with open roster spots. One of them is from Christian Hanselman, a swim coach and recruiting coordinator at BYU.

When my time came to start my recruiting process, BYU made all the requirements I was looking for within a team. I strive to find balance in my academics, swimming, and religion, and BYU, from what I could tell from my research, was one of the few places where I could thrive in all three of these aspects. Above that, the swimmers who competed for BYU showed impressive improvement within only months of them joining the team. This made me excited about my potential career at the team.

When I got offered a spot on the team it was one of the best and worst days of my life. On the one hand, I achieved one of my biggest dreams ever, and on the other hand, I was going to say goodbye to my family in less than three months. A lot of mixed emotions were flowing through my head at this time—not just for me but for my whole family.

As an international student, there are concerns from all aspects of our lives-mainly academic and personal. South African education and syllabus is different from the American education and syllabus, and therefore, the adaptation to the new education will be hard. With that being said, being in a completely different country from your family does not help you sleep at night either. There is a constant concern that something might happen to them while you are here. Above this, new NCAA rules and roster caps have made it really hard to get a spot on a team and to keep it as well. The thought of the possibility of being cut from the team is haunting. But it also motivates me to keep pushing myself to prove not only to myself but to the coaching staff at BYU that I deserve my spot on the team.

As a competitive athlete, being in an environment where I am not the best drives me to be a better athlete and teammate. I believe that every person goes through phases in their lives, and once you outgrow a certain phase, you must make the hard decision of deciding to move on to a new phase. For me, the hard decision was leaving my family and friends behind while I go pursue greatness in a different country that will offer me new and greater challenges that will allow me to grow as a person, athlete, and friend—allowing me to achieve a new level of greatness.
